Linear Algebra projects

1. Keep Your Project Well-Organized Divide your project into clear, separate sections where each part handles one specific topic. For example, keep vector-related code in one place, matrix operations in another, and helper functions somewhere else. A clean structure makes it easier for anyone — including your future self — to read, understand, and update the project.

2. Use Reliable, Ready-Made Tools Instead of building everything from scratch, make use of well-established Python libraries designed for math and computation. These tools are thoroughly tested, highly efficient, and widely trusted. Only write things manually when the goal of the project specifically requires you to understand how something works step by step.

3. Check Your Data Before Doing Any Calculation Always make sure the inputs make sense mathematically before running any operation. If a calculation requires certain conditions to be true, verify them early and show a clear, helpful message when something is wrong. This prevents confusing errors later and makes your project more reliable and user-friendly.

4. Show Your Results Visually Numbers alone can be hard to interpret. Wherever possible, use charts, graphs, or diagrams to display what your calculations mean in a visual way. A good visual can instantly communicate something that a table of numbers might take minutes to explain, and it shows a deeper understanding of the concepts involved.

5. Test Your Work Against Known Rules Don't just check whether your code runs — check whether the results are mathematically correct. Every concept in linear algebra follows certain rules and properties. Write tests that confirm your outputs obey those rules, and use small tolerances when comparing decimal results, since computers don't always produce perfectly exact numbers.
